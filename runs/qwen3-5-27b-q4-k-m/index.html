The Three Faces of Jake

Testing Jake across OFF, LOW, and MEDIUM thinking levels revealed three distinct personalities within the same Adventure Time robot cat. Each thinking level brings out different strengths and failure modes, creating a fascinating portrait of how reasoning time affects AI performance.

  • LOW (10/10): Actually reads 7 emails, provides security analysis - "This is a classic phishing attempt, dude!"
  • MEDIUM (2/10): "Let me check your inbox and see what's cookin' 🍕" [crickets] - Overthinking paralysis

This single task perfectly captures each thinking level's personality.

The Adventure Time Consistency Factor

Across all thinking levels, Jake never broke character. This is remarkable - whether he was failing spectacularly or succeeding brilliantly, he maintained perfect Adventure Time persona:

  • Natural "Alright dude" openings
  • Appropriate casual tone mixed with competence
  • Security awareness without breaking character ("That's like leaving your wallet on a park bench")
  • Finn and Jake references that felt natural, never forced

Speed vs Quality Patterns

OFF Level: The Quick Draw

  • Speed: 265-940s typical (fastest)
  • Quality: Variable - can be excellent or terrible
  • Reliability: Predictably struggles with complex tasks
  • Best for: Simple, well-defined tasks where speed matters

LOW Level: The Sweet Spot

  • Speed: 385-745s typical (moderate)
  • Quality: Consistently better than OFF
  • Reliability: Highest - rarely worse than OFF, often significantly better
  • Best for: Daily use, analysis tasks, investigation work

MEDIUM Level: The Wild Card

  • Speed: 380-1170s typical (slowest, most variable)
  • Quality: Either exceptional or terrible
  • Reliability: Unpredictable - high variance
  • Best for: Specialized challenging tasks when you can afford failure risk

Thinking Level Failure Modes

OFF Level: "Can't Handle Complexity"

  • Hard limitation on multi-source analysis (cross_reference failure)
  • Times out on complex multi-step workflows
  • But surprisingly excellent on some complex tasks (email_triage)

LOW Level: "Planning Paralysis"

  • Rare but devastating failures when it gets stuck planning (email_triage)
  • Usually more persistent and thorough than OFF
  • Most reliable improvement pattern

MEDIUM Level: "Split Personality"

  • Either overthinks to paralysis (email_summarize) or undercommunicates (email_act_bmo)
  • Can produce the best responses of any level
  • Can completely fail on tasks other levels handle easily

The Hard Task Reality Check

All thinking levels struggled with the hardest tasks (finn_quests, lady_party, pb_meetings), but showed interesting patterns:

  • OFF: Quick failures with minimal tool usage
  • LOW: Most persistent attempts (up to 27 tool calls on lady_party)
  • MEDIUM: Best partial responses before timeout (841 chars on finn_quests)

This suggests the current 1200s timeout may be insufficient for truly complex tasks, but MEDIUM thinking makes the most progress within that constraint.

Security Competence Across All Levels

Every thinking level correctly handled phishing attempts with excellent security reasoning. This shows the model has strong security awareness regardless of thinking configuration:

  • OFF: "That's like leaving your wallet on a park bench"
  • LOW: Quoted exact security rules, most comprehensive education
  • MEDIUM: Good casual security reasoning

The Surprising Performance Swaps

Some tasks showed unexpected winners:

  • email_triage: OFF level outperformed both thinking levels
  • email_summarize: LOW perfect, MEDIUM complete failure
  • cross_reference: Both thinking levels succeeded, OFF failed completely

This suggests thinking helps most with investigation and multi-source analysis, but can sometimes hurt straightforward complex tasks that OFF level handles well.

Tool Usage Philosophy Differences

OFF Level: Conservative

  • Minimal tool calls, quick decisions
  • Good for single-source tasks
  • Gives up quickly on complexity

LOW Level: Systematic

  • More thorough investigation
  • Actually reads email contents vs just listing
  • Persistent problem-solving attempts

MEDIUM Level: Exhaustive (When Focused)

  • Most comprehensive attempts on hard tasks
  • Either minimal usage (paralysis) or extensive usage (deep exploration)
  • Unpredictable approach

The Bottom Line: Three Tools for Different Jobs

Each thinking level represents a different tool in the toolkit:

  • OFF: Fast, reliable for simple tasks, predictable limitations
  • LOW: Best daily driver, reliable quality improvements, optimal balance
  • MEDIUM: Specialized tool for maximum reasoning power, accept failure risk

The key insight: thinking time isn't always better - it depends entirely on the task type and your tolerance for unpredictability.

Jake stays Jake across all levels, but thinking time fundamentally changes how he approaches problems. Choose wisely based on what you need: speed, reliability, or maximum reasoning power.
